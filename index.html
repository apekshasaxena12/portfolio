Multi-agent AI assistant

A supervisor watches every message and hands it to the right specialist. Below: live routing.

router demo

What I built
  • A multi-agent AI assistant with LangGraph-orchestrated routing across six specialized agents — coding, data analytics, SQL querying, document RAG, web search, and voice — all behind a single chat interface, so users never switch modes.
  • A local RAG server (FastAPI + FAISS) enabling grounded document summarization, Q&A, and cross-document comparison, with responses streaming in real time over WebSocket.
  • A multi-agent document-discussion platform where two AI agents debate a document and narrate podcast-style summaries — I extended it with multi-document support.
  • A hands-free Voice Chat mode on top of that platform: speech-to-text input and dual-voice narration through Edge TTS, so users can discuss documents without touching the keyboard.
Under the hood
  • A LangGraph supervisor inspects each incoming message and hands it to the right specialist agent; results stream back into one conversation thread.
  • Inference runs on Groq-hosted LLaMA and Gemma models plus Google Gemini, behind a Django backend with a React frontend.

Industry news, answerable

live dashboard demo
crude margins tighten in Q3 ··· refinery throughput up 4.2% ··· new pipeline commissioned in Gujarat ··· LNG spot prices ease ··· biofuel blending target raised ···
What I built
  • A natural-language Q&A system over oil & gas industry news — users ask questions and get grounded, cited answers instead of manually tracking multiple news sources.
  • A daily auto-generated summary report covering all tracked industry news, delivered without anyone having to ask.
  • On-demand summarization of any article URL or PDF a user drops in — paste a link, get the gist.
  • A live dashboard where users see the day's oil & gas developments and query them directly, without ever touching the underlying pipeline.
Under the hood
  • Tracked articles are embedded with Sentence Transformers and indexed in Qdrant; questions retrieve the most relevant coverage before any answer is written.
  • Answers are generated locally by Llama 3.2 via Ollama and grounded in the retrieved articles — every claim traces back to a source, served through FastAPI to a React frontend.
